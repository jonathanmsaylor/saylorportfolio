Steven has over 25 years in the movie industry and has successfully completed over 70 projects, which include television (over 400 episodes) and features. His past experiences have allowed him to bring something different and unique to his Set Designer career. He is well respected in the film industry and highly sought after for his innovative designs and steadfast work ethic.

Steven started his first architectural design company when he was 18 years old. At this time Steven was honing his skills in architectural design and managing clients, contractors, engineers and city county officals.

Steven went on to work at a civil engineering firm to expand his knowledge in other areas. These included street and utility design, hillside design and construction and earthquake retrofitting. He then obtained his general contracting license from the State of California and began operating his own architectural design and construction company. The firm quickly grew to include the managing of employees, enigneers and construction crews. Steven's firm successfully designed and completed over 700 projects.

In 1991 Steven joined MCA/Universal Studios immediately after the infamous New York Street fire. Steven began designing part of the new backlot while being tasked to use his knowledge in design and construction to work as a project manager for permanent studio projects. These projects included studio infrastructure, hillside stabilization, office construction, building relocation and managing the construction and permitting of an approximately 1m square feet, 10 story building to include concrete and steel construction.

While at MCA/universal studios Steven took on the role of Damage Assesment Team leader and was trained by the State of California in post earthquake damage assessment. After the 1994 Northridge earthquake Steven was deputized by the county of Los Angeles as a building inspector with the purpose of assessing the safety and structural integrity of buildings. A new opportunity arose and Steven joined 20th Century Fox as a project manager. Utilizing his knowledge in structure, concrete and steel construction. He coordinated projects such as the new Fox Broadcasting building (managing the capping of 25 oil wells), a parking structure and the new Crafts building.
